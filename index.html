Salary increment month

In India, appraisals are typically in April and new salaries take effect from May 1. Adjust if your employer follows a different cycle. This only affects salary; expenses and investments use the default April cycle.

New salary visible from the month after this (e.g. April → effective May 1)
How increment timing works. If you onboard before April 1, your inputs reflect the current year's salary — the first increment lands in May of this year. If you onboard on or after April 1, it's assumed you're entering next year's salary (since the April payout happened) — so the first increment lands in May of next year. You can override the appraisal month above.

No. 05Wealth

Your investments today.

Current balance for each instrument plus your typical monthly contribution. Mark each one as liquid or illiquid — you can adjust anytime.

Note. Investments are wealth-building instruments — Mutual Funds, FDs, PPF, Stocks, Gold, etc. Your day-to-day cash (Salary & Savings accounts) goes in the next step. "Liquid" means you can access the money quickly without material loss; equity funds and crypto are usually liquid, PPF and real estate aren't.
No. 06Day-to-day

Your accounts.

The everyday accounts you transact from — salary credit, credit card, anything else. Liabilities (like credit cards) take their balance as the amount you currently owe; Ledger displays it as a negative on the Today page.

How cash accounts work. All your asset accounts (Salary, Savings, etc.) earn the same interest rate, set on the Rates page (default 4% p.a.). Your monthly surplus lands in the Savings account in projections. Liabilities like credit cards reduce your net worth.
No. 07Assumptions

Expected returns.

The annualized rate each investment is expected to earn. Defaults reflect conservative long-run averages; adjust to your own view.

How growth works. Salary & expense growth rates compound annually — your January salary in year 2 is your January salary in year 1 × (1 + rate). Investment returns and cash interest compound monthly. Recurring one-offs (bonuses, annual expenses) grow at the salary or expense rate respectively. Market returns are long-term averages, not guarantees.

About the engine

Ledger derives every figure from your onboarding inputs. Monthly income, expenses, EMIs, and scheduled contributions are projected across the horizon. Balances are compounded monthly using the annualized rates above.

For each month the engine computes: total income − EMIs − expenses = net surplus. Scheduled monthly contributions flow into each investment. Any residual flows into the Savings account. Each asset earns interest on its running balance.

All figures indicative. Ledger is not a regulated financial advisor; consult a professional for material decisions.
